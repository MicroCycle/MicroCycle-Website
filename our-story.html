Our Journey

From Problem to Solution

The story of how MicroCycle came to be

Discovering the Problem

It all started in our university maker space. We noticed bins overflowing with failed 3D prints, support structures, and test pieces. The waste was staggering—and it was all ending up in landfills. We knew there had to be a better way.

Customer Interviews

We spoke with dozens of makers, educators, and makerspace managers. The feedback was unanimous: everyone wanted a solution for their PLA waste, but existing options were either too expensive, too large, or too complex. We validated that this problem was real and widespread.

Research & Experimentation

We dove deep into material science, testing different temperatures, pressures, and combinations. After countless experiments (and more than a few failures), we discovered that combining shredded PLA with acrylic under heat created beautiful, sturdy sheets perfect for laser cutting.

Building the Prototype

With our process validated, we designed and built the first MicroCycler prototype. It was rough around the edges, but it worked! We could transform waste PLA into gorgeous custom sheets right in our workshop.

Refining the Design

Feedback from early testers helped us refine every aspect of the machine—from the heating elements to the user interface. We focused on making it compact, affordable, and easy enough for anyone to use.

Where We Are Today

Today, MicroCycle is ready to bring sustainable 3D printing to maker spaces everywhere. Our solution stays in the shop—and keeps the waste with it. We're excited to help makers close the loop on plastic waste.

Our Mission

"We believe that sustainability and creativity go hand in hand. Our mission is to transform 3D printing waste from a problem into an opportunity—empowering makers to create beautiful new materials while protecting our planet."
